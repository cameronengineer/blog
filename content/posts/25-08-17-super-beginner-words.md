Dreaming Spanish is a video platform that provides comprehensible input videos for spanish language learners. The core principle is that language acquisition through massive exposure to content can teach you a language in the same way we learnt your native language. [This method is detailed on their website, and it actively discourages rote memorization](https://www.dreamingspanish.com/method).

While the theory is sounds good, I've found that my learning style benefits from some supplemental study. This has led me back to a familiar tool: rote learning flashcards.

To create a more effective set of flashcards, I decided to find the most common words used in the [Dreaming Spanish Super Beginner videos](https://www.dreamingspanish.com/browse?sort=easy&level=superbeginner). The top 300 words have been counted and are summarized in the table below.

As expected, the most frequent words are functional stop words. While essential for grammar, they can make for tedious flashcards.
